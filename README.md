## What Is Liquid Ketamine?
Liquid ketamine, often referred to as ketamine hydrochloride in injectable form, is a dissociative anesthetic used primarily in medical and veterinary settings. It induces a trance-like state, providing pain relief and sedation. Developed in the 1960s, it's valued for its rapid onset and minimal respiratory depression compared to other anesthetics.

In 2026, ketamine has gained attention for potential off-label uses, such as treating depression via esketamine nasal sprays (FDA-approved since 2019). However, liquid forms are typically reserved for clinical administration and are not available over-the-counter.

## Legal Ways to Obtain Liquid Ketamine
Ketamine is strictly regulated. For legitimate medical needs, it can only be acquired through licensed channels:

### Medical Prescriptions and Clinics
- **Veterinary Use**: Liquid ketamine is commonly used in animal care. Veterinarians can prescribe it for procedures.
- **Human Medicine**: In hospitals or clinics, it's administered by professionals for anesthesia or pain management. For depression treatment, esketamine (a derivative) is available via prescription at specialized clinics.
- **How to Access**: Schedule an appointment with a doctor or psychiatrist. In the US, programs like those under the FDA allow supervised use. Internationally, check local regulations—e.g., in the EU, it's controlled under similar schedules.

### Compounding Pharmacies
Authorized pharmacies can prepare liquid ketamine under a doctor's prescription for specific therapeutic needs. This requires a valid medical reason and oversight.

Never attempt to buy liquid ketamine from unregulated sources, as it may be counterfeit or contaminated, posing severe health risks.
